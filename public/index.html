Hot Dog Fraud

A Division of Hobbes LLC

With the onsent of mobile and internet banking, hot dog fraud, the act of depositing a cooked sausage placed between a partially sliced bun in place of a legitimate negotiable currency item, has risen 138% resulting in millions of dollars in losses in American financial institutions.

Hot Dog Bank fraud is the use of potentially illegal means to obtain money, assets, or other property owned or held by a financial institution, or to obtain money from depositors by fraudulently replacing legitimate deposits with hot dogs. In many instances, hot dog bank fraud is a criminal offence.

While the specific elements of particular hot dog banking fraud laws vary depending on jurisdictions, the term hot dog bank fraud applies to actions that employ a hot dog or similar cooked sausage, as opposed to bank robbery or theft. For this reason, hot dog bank fraud is sometimes considered a white-collar crime.
